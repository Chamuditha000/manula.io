About Me

I am a final-year BSc (Hons) Physics undergraduate at the University of Ruhuna (2020–2025), with a strong focus on computational physics, accelerator physics, and physics-based computer simulations. I enjoy building interactive 3D environments in Unity to visualize real-world phenomena, especially accelerator and beam dynamics, and I am interested in integrating image processing and machine learning into experimental physics and educational tools.

Research & Projects

  • Interactive Accelerator Simulation Game (2025) – Real-time 3D interface built with Unity and Blender to simulate proton acceleration from LINAC4 through subsequent accelerator stages, inspired by the CERN accelerator complex.
    Related Certification: Introduction to Particle Accelerators – Lund University
  • Simulating Negative Hydrogen Ion Acceleration in LINAC-4 (2024) – Educational 3D simulation platform presented at BCVSIP 2024, visualizing key technical aspects of LINAC4 using Unity 3D.
    Related Certification: Introduction to Particle Accelerators – Lund University
  • Image Processing Algorithm for Tea Concentration (2025) – RGB-based regression model to estimate BOPF tea concentration; presented at the International Tea Symposium (InTSym100) 2025.

  • Travelling Microscope – Design and Fabrication (2024) – Low-cost, robust travelling microscope for undergraduate labs, designed in AutoCAD and fabricated with focus on accuracy and durability.
